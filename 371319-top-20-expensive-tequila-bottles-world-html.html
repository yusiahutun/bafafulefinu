How is tequila made?

The blue agave plant is distilled in wooden casts for a few months to a few years. The plant's central stem is then cut out, steam cooked and fermented. Before being bottled, the fermented product is distilled at least twice.

After the pinas are steamed, the sweet juice is crushed from the stems then yeast is added to kick start the fermentation process. The tepache or juice from the baked pinas is fermented in containers until 5% or 10 proof alcohol is attained.

The mixture is distilled again to raise the alcohol content to 55% or 110 proof. Water is then added during bottling to reduce the alcohol content to between 76 and 80 proof.

What makes a good tequila?

The best quality of this spirit is processed from 100% Weber blue agave juice. Using cheap ingredients will lower its taste and quality.

Types of tequilas

There are 5 types of tequilas based on the ageing period and process.

  • Tipo Uno/Tequila Blanco: Aged for up to 60 days, the spirit is clear, cheap, and the best for mixing.
  • Tipo Dos/Joven Tequila/golden tequila/tequila Oro: Tequila Blanco with added flavours and sugar syrup or other sweeteners to enhance the taste.
  • Tipo Tres/Tequila Reposado: Aged for periods of between two and nine months in wooden casks, giving it an oaky flavour.
  • Tipo cuatro/Tequila Anejo: Aged from between 18 months and three years, giving it a darker and robust appearance, with balanced oak and agave flavour.
  • Tipo Cinco/Extra-Anejo Tequila: Aged for more than three years, giving it the richest aroma and best for sipping.
