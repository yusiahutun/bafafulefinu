What is a Vitamin C Serum and Its Benefits

As mentioned earlier, vitamin C is a potent antioxidant known for its many health benefits. But, over the last decade, topical use of vitamin C has gained immense popularity. Now you can find small dark bottles of vitamin C serums on almost everyone's bathroom shelves and vanity. There are multiple reasons for this phenomenon.  

It is now well known that antioxidants reverse the damage caused by free radicals and rejuvenate skin. However, no other antioxidant comes close to what vitamin C can do for your skin. It can be attributed to the fact that it is one of the “most abundant” antioxidants in our skin. Several studies suggest this incredible ingredient, when applied topically, not only offers protection against free radical damage, but also promotes collagen, makes skin soft, supple, and plump, offers photoprotection against both UVA and UVB rays, reduces hyperpigmentation by inhibiting melanin, evens skin tone and texture, minimizes fine lines, and brightens skin (1), making it one of the most dermatologist-recommended products. 

Vitamin C comes in a variety of forms, such as L-ascorbic acid (which happens to be the most studied form), ascorbyl palmitate, ethyl ascorbic acid, ascorbyl glucoside, and sodium ascorbyl phosphate, to name a few (2). Different forms have different benefits and efficacy. While some forms of vitamin C can be quite harsh, other forms are known to be gentle and more stable. It makes using topical vitamin C serums for sensitive and acne-prone skin more convenient.

Why Use a Vitamin C Serum for Acne-Prone Skin?

Vitamin C for acne-prone skin works better than you can imagine, thanks to its anti-inflammatory action. It promotes the synthesis of ceramide in our skin, which forms a crucial part of the outer layer of the skin, thereby reducing redness and inflammation. It also strengthens the skin barrier, which prevents irritants and acne-causing bacteria from getting in. This effectively reduces the frequency and severity of breakouts (3). And that's not all! It boosts the cell renewal process to reduce the appearance of post-acne scars and hyperpigmentation to give you flawless and glowing skin with each use (4). So, come on and get on the vitamin C bandwagon and watch your skin transform. Keep reading to get to know more!

    How to Choose the Best Vitamin C Serums for Acne-prone Skin?

    You now know the benefits of vitamin C for acne-prone skin. With our recommendations, you can easily pick your ideal match but there's still more to know! Here are a few aspects you must consider to make an informed choice: 

    Skin Type

    Consider your skin type, whether you have an oily skin type or a dry skin type. Any skin type can have acne, so you have to be mindful of it when buying a vitamin C serum. Choose a formulation that suits your skin type and needs. 

    Ingredients

    Read the label carefully and check if the serum contains ingredients that may irritate your skin or cause allergies. Look for ingredients like aloe vera, niacinamide, ferulic acids, vitamin E, vitamin B5, centella asiatica, amino acids, and ceramides, for added benefits. A combination of vitamin C, vitamin E, and ferulic acid, like the SkinCeuticals C E Ferulic serum, is rather popular for its antioxidant benefits. Some formulations also contain glycolic acid and salicylic acid for added benefits.

    Forms of Vitamin C 

    Get acquainted with the various forms of vitamin C and choose the more gentle one. You may also consult a board-certified dermatologist to get personalized recommendations on the form of vitamin C that will suit you the best.

    Concentration 

    Consider the concentration of your vitamin C serum. As a beginner, start low and slow. You may begin with 5% or 10% and gradually increase the concentration, but only if needed. Keep in mind that more concentration doesn't mean more benefits.

    Formula

    Look for a pH-balanced and stable formula to make the most out of your vitamin C serum. 

    Packaging

    It is an important aspect as vitamin C is prone to oxidation. So, you must choose a serum in an opaque or dark-tinted glass bottle to protect it from light and air. 

    Customer Reviews

    Read up on the customer reviews and feedback to gain insights into the efficacy and performance of the product.

    How to Use Vitamin C Serum for Acne-prone Skin?

    Here's a step-by-step guide on how to effectively use a vitamin C serum for acne-prone skin for optimal results:

    • Start with a clean face. If you use a toner, then now is the time to apply one.
    • Some formulations of vitamin C serums can be applied on a damp face while some require to be applied on a dry face. Be mindful of that.
    • Take a little amount of the serum on your fingertips or the palm of your hand. Remember that less is more. you only need to evenly cover your face with the serum and not drench your skin in it.
    • It is advisable to do a patch test about 24 hours before using it on your whole face. This will allow you to check for any adverse reactions
    • Don't use a serum that smells bad or has turned cloudy or orange. These are signs of oxidation and when used on the skin, such a serum can irritate your skin instead of doing it good.
    • Be gentle with the application. Focus on areas that require more attention, like your acne scars.
    • Consult your dermatologist before using it on active acne.
    • Allow the serum to fully absorb before moving on to the next step in your skincare routine.
    • Follow up with a non comedogenic moisturizer. You can skip this step if your sunscreen feels moisturizing enough.
    • Don't forget to wear sunscreen.
    • Don't mix vitamin C serum with other products. Avoid using it in the same routine as benzoyl peroxide, AHAs, BHAs, and retinol to prevent irritation or any potential adverse reaction. It is fine if these are included within the serum because the formulators have taken care of things.

    FAQ's

    Will vitamin C irritate acne-prone skin?

    No, vitamin C does not irritate acne-prone skin. Instead, it helps reduce the irritation and inflammation. However, certain formulas and ingredients may cause irritation which is why always do a patch test before applying a serum to your face.

    What’s the difference between dark spots/marks and hyperpigmentation?

    Hyperpigmentation is a common skin condition in which the skin or patches of skin become more pigmented than the rest. It can be seen in the form of post-acne marks, blemishes, dark spots, freckles, or sun spots.

    Is vitamin C serum better than hyaluronic acid?

    Vitamin C serum has more benefits than hyaluronic acid but which is better will depend on your individual needs and skin requirements.

    Do you put vitamin C serum on before or after moisturizing?

    Vitamin C serum must be used before moisturizing.

    What is the most effective form of vitamin C?

    The most effective and the most studied form of vitamin C is L-ascorbic acid.

    Does vitamin C cause acne breakout?

    No, it does not cause acne breakouts but works at preventing them.

    Can we use vitamin C serum for oily acne-prone skin?

    Yes, you can use vitamin C serums for oily acne-prone skin.

    Do I need a moisturizer after vitamin C serum?

    It depends on how moisturizing your serum and sunscreen are. You don’t necessarily have to use a moisturizer after.

    How often should I use vitamin C serum for acne-prone skin?

    You can use the vitamin C serum for acne-prone skin daily in your am routine.

    How long does it take to see results when using vitamin C serum for acne scars?

    You may start seeing certain effects within a week of use but for visible results for acne scars, you may have to wait somewhere between 8 to 12 weeks.
