The most shocking moments from racy reality show Joseline's Cabaret

It's the most outrageous and offensive reality show that's ever been made. 

And viewers have been left shocked by Joseline's Cabaret, which follows former stripper and Love & Hip-Hop: Atlanta star Joseline Hernandez as she puts together a racy cabaret show featuring a rowdy cast of exotic dancers and sex workers.

The X-rated series, which is available on the streaming app Zeus, is too hot for TV and contains graphic sex scenes, nudity, violent brawls, racial slurs, and the kinds of politically incorrect remarks that would get most mainstream celebrities canceled in this day and age.

It comes just weeks after leading lady Hernandez, dubbed 'the Puerto Rican Princess', was arrested for brawling with police officers in Florida after assaulting her former co-star, rapper Big Lex.

As Joseline's Cabaret prepares to return for its fourth season, Daily Mail Australia takes a look back at some of the show's most shocking and offensive moments.

At first she performed witchcraft on the women, before taking to the stage in a virginal white robe with a bottle of holy water in hand.

White cast member repeatedly uses the N-word

Outside of the physical fighting and graphic nudity, one of the most controversial elements of Joseline's Cabaret is the show's liberal use of slurs and offensive language.

'Like girl, if you were ever offended you would've corrected me the same f***ing day, cos b***h this is how I talk! You're not gonna educate me,' she added.

After refusing to stop using the word, Amber pounced on Diamond and punched her in the head repeatedly before daring her to continue using the slur.

ReRe alleged that K Kapri had been told to choose between starting a fight with either Lexi Blow or Ms Wet Wet, and if she didn't then she'd kicked off the show.

'She's a sweetheart but they just wanted her to look that evil,' ReRe said of Kapri.

However, other cast members have disputed ReRe's allegations, insisting that the fights occur naturally due to having so many big personalities in a small space together.
